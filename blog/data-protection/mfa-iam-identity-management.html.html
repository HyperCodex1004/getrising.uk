Multi-Factor Authentication combines something you know (password) with something you have (smartphone, token) or something you are (biometrics). It drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access, even if passwords are compromised.

In 2025, MFA is expected as a baseline control for:

  • Employee and contractor access to internal systems
  • Customer portals
  • Administrator accounts
  • Third-party integrations

Many regulations, including GDPR, NIS2, and DORA, now strongly recommend or indirectly require MFA to demonstrate "appropriate" security measures.
