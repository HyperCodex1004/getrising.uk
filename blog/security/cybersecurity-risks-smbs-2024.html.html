Top Cybersecurity Risks for SMBs in 2024 (And How to Tackle Them)

  • Laurentiu Mihaila
  • Apr 15, 2024

In today‚Äôs fast-evolving digital landscape, Small and Medium Businesses (SMBs) face mounting cybersecurity threats. Unlike large enterprises, SMBs often operate with lean IT resources, making them attractive targets for cybercriminals.

If you're an SMB owner, IT manager, or business leader, understanding the top cybersecurity risks you face in 2025 is critical to safeguarding your organization.

üö®SMBs Under Attack

  • 43% of all cyberattacks now target SMBs. (Source: Verizon DBIR 2023)
  • 61% of SMBs experienced a cyberattack in the past year alone.
  • The average cost of a successful attack on an SMB exceeds ¬£100,000.

    • Why are SMBs attractive to cybercriminals?

    SMBs frequently lack the advanced cybersecurity defenses of large enterprises, positioning them as prime targets for cybercriminals. Limited IT and security resources hinder their ability to detect and respond to threats effectively. Slow patch management leaves critical vulnerabilities exposed, while inconsistent Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) policies further weaken their security posture. This combination of resource constraints and security gaps makes SMBs highly susceptible to ransomware, phishing, and other cyberattacks.

    ‚ö† The Top 3 Cybersecurity Risks for SMBs

    1. Phishing & Social Engineering

    Phishing remains the #1 attack vector for SMBs globally.

    • 80% of SMB breaches begin with a phishing email.
    • Business Email Compromise (BEC) and fake invoice scams are increasingly common.
    • Employees are often tricked into clicking malicious links or sharing credentials.

    Mitigation Tips:

    • Conduct regular phishing simulations and awareness training.
    • Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) across all accounts.
    • Deploy email security tools to block malicious content.

    2. BYOD & Shadow IT

    With the rise of remote and hybrid work, many employees now use personal devices and unauthorized apps for business purposes, often outside IT‚Äôs control. This creates Shadow IT.

    • 95% of organizations now permit some form of BYOD.
    • 62% of cybersecurity experts cite data leakage as a major concern with BYOD.
    • 43% of employees have been targeted with work-related phishing on personal devices.

    Mitigation Tips:

    • Develop a BYOD policy that defines clear security requirements.
    • Use Mobile Device Management (MDM) to enforce device compliance.
    • Monitor for Shadow IT activity with Cloud Access Security Brokers (CASB).

    ‚û° Learn more in our deep-dive post: BYOD: A Blessing and a Curse

    ‚û° Explore: Shadow IT - Expanding Your Attack Surface

    3. Ransomware

    Ransomware has become the most financially devastating threat for SMBs.

    • 66% of SMBs were hit by ransomware in 2023.
    • Average ransom demands are increasing, often exceeding ¬£125,000.
    • Double-extortion tactics are on the rise (encrypting data and threatening to leak it).

    Mitigation Tips:

    • Maintain offline backups and test restore procedures regularly.
    • Apply zero-trust security models to limit lateral movement inside your network.
    • Patch systems promptly and deploy endpoint detection & response (EDR) tools.

    ‚û° Explore our dedicated article: The Ransomware Survival Guide for SMBs

    üåê Emerging Threat: Supply Chain Attacks

    While less frequent than the top 3, supply chain attacks are rising fast.

    In 2023, 48% of SMBs were indirectly affected by a third-party data breach.

    üí° The SMB Cybersecurity Challenge

    Unlike large enterprises with dedicated SOCs and security teams, SMBs often lack:

    • In-house cybersecurity expertise.
    • A fully-developed incident response plan.
    • Formal governance over personal device and cloud app usage.

    ‚úÖ So, What Can SMBs Do?

    1. Adopt a security-first culture: Train employees regularly on cyber hygiene.
    2. Invest in essential tools: MFA, backup solutions, MDM, and endpoint security.
    3. Enforce clear IT policies: Especially for BYOD and Shadow IT practices.
    4. Leverage external partners: Work with MSPs or MSSPs to bolster defenses.

    Cybersecurity for SMBs in 2025 is no longer optional ‚Äî it's essential. By addressing these top risks, you can significantly reduce your vulnerability and protect your business, your data, and your customers.
